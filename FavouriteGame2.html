Description of Elden Ring

Elden Ring is an epic action RPG developed by FromSoftware and published by Bandai Namco Entertainment. Released in 2022, this game stands out for its challenging gameplay, rich lore, and expansive open-world exploration.

Set in the mystical Lands Between, the story begins with the shattering of the great Elden Ring. This event, known as the Shattering, was triggered by the Night of the Black Knives, when Godwyn the Golden, a demigod and Marika's offspring, was the first to perish. Following this, the demigods claimed the shards of the Elden Ring, resulting in a war that left the realm in ruins.

Now, Queen Marika the Eternal has disappeared, and the land is in chaos. The player, known as a Tarnished, is called upon to cross the fog and stand before the Elden Ring to become the Elden Lord. Along the journey, players will encounter formidable foes, including Hoarah Loux, chieftain of the badlands; the ever-brilliant Goldmask; Fia, the Deathbed Companion; the loathsome Dung Eater; and Sir Gideon Ofnir, the All-knowing.

As with other games by FromSoftware, Elden Ring is known for its punishing yet rewarding gameplay. Players must master the combat mechanics and explore every corner of the vast world to uncover its secrets and progress through the story.

Why Elden Ring Stands Out

One of the most commendable aspects of Elden Ring is its commitment to a single-purchase model. Unlike live-service games, which are often riddled with microtransactions and pay-to-win mechanics, Elden Ring respects players by offering a complete game experience without hidden costs.

In an industry where big corporations frequently prioritize monetization over player experience, Elden Ring is a breath of fresh air. It's a game made to be played and completed, not milked for continuous revenue. There are no subscription fees, no in-game currency to buy with real money, and no paywalls hindering your progress.

Hidetaka Miyazaki, the president of FromSoftware, eloquently addresses this in an interview with The Guardian. He explains, "If we really wanted the whole world to play the game, we could just crank the difficulty down more and more. But that wasn't the right approach. The sense of achievement that players gain from overcoming these hurdles is such a fundamental part of the experience. Turning down difficulty would strip the game of that joy – which, in my eyes, would break the game itself."

This approach stands in stark contrast to the practices of many modern game developers, who often strip away players' rights to truly own the games they purchase. These companies release games as live services, constantly demanding more money from players while providing a fraction of the enjoyment.

Moreover, the ever-present threat of games being pulled from servers with little to no notice further diminishes the value of live-service titles. Players are left with nothing, their investments evaporated. In contrast, when you buy Elden Ring, you own the game. You get to explore its world, master its challenges, and enjoy its story without the fear of losing access.

For these reasons and more, Elden Ring is not just a game; it’s a statement against the prevailing trends of the gaming industry. It represents a return to form, where player satisfaction and game integrity are paramount. And that, in my view, is what makes Elden Ring truly great.
